About

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Miyazaki. Although my background started in electronics, I transitioned into computer science about five years ago, focusing on machine learning and computer vision. During my Master’s, I worked on a project where I developed an intelligent irrigation system for strawberry cultivation. This project combined IoT and machine learning to optimize water usage and detect nutrient deficiencies. Later, for my Ph.D., I expanded into elderly care, designing a real-time monitoring and behavior analysis system using stereo depth cameras and deep learning for action recognition. This journey from electronics to computer science has been both challenging and rewarding. It’s helped me develop the ability to quickly adapt and apply new concepts. I’ve also been working hard to strengthen my knowledge of computer science fundamentals to support my hands-on experience. I’m excited about the opportunity to contribute what I’ve learned so far and continue growing in this field.

  • Programming: Proficient in Python for data analysis, with working knowledge of MATLAB and C for software development.
  • AI and Machine Learning: Skilled in frameworks such as TensorFlow and PyTorch for developing intelligent systems.
  • Computer Vision: Expertise in OpenCV, human action recognition, and stereo vision integration for analyzing image and video data.
  • IoT and Electronics: Experienced with Arduino and Raspberry Pi for designing and implementing sensor-based systems.
  • Additional: Knowledge of generative AI, multimodal data (text, images, audio, and video) fusion, and time-series modeling for problem-solving.

Looking for an opportunity to work in a challenging position in academic or industry combining my skills in Electronics Engineering and Computer Science.
